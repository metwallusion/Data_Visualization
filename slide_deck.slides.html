Prosper loans dataset Investigation¶

by Ahmed Metwalli¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, there are two key points to be taken into account:

  • Which features can predict default on credit.
  • Which features is ,actually, influencing 'ProsperRating (Alpha)'.

Dataset Overview¶

Data set provided with shape of [113937,81] this means 133937 loans and 81 features. For this investigation, these features are taken into account: Term, LoanStatus, BorrowerRate, ProsperRating (Alpha), ListingCategory (numeric), EmploymentStatus, DelinquenciesLast7Years, StatedMonthlyIncome, TotalProsperLoans, LoanOriginalAmount, LoanOriginationDate, Recommendations, Investors . After the wrangling process (Gather, Assess and Clean), there are 26005 data points are extracted to be used during the investigation.

Wrangling Phase¶

The issues are as follows:

  1. Prosper has used ProsperRating (Alpha) since 2009 ,unlike before, when Prosper had used credit grade. As a conseuqence I just kept the data since 2009
  2. A tidiness issue is that most of the loans in dataset are current loans and doesn't give information on how default on credit can be predicted. 'Defaulted' credits were represented in two categries 'Chargedoff and 'Defaulted'. A quick solve for this issue is done which is to remove all the current loans and merge 'Chargedoff' with 'Defaulted' credits.
  3. 'StatedMonthlyIncome' features had 2 main problems: Many outliers and very large range out of the triple of the standard deviation from the mean. Obviously, it is solved by dropping the out of range (out of triple the standard deviation).

During the wrangling phase we extracted 26005 loans from the data of which we have 19664 'Completed' loans and 6341 'Defaulted'.

In the end, after the wrangling process, we notice that the shape of 'StatedMonthlyIncome' looks like if it is normally distributed with high right skewness and a mean of 5328 USD with a maximum of 30416.67 USD.

From data dictionary we find that, 'ProsperRating (Alpha)' is an ordinal categorical variable with the following values from the highest to lowest ['AA', 'A', 'B', 'C', 'D', 'E', 'HR']. This feature 'ProsperRating (Alpha)' has almost normal distribution of which the data is centered on 'C'and (the edges 'AA' and 'HR') are less likely to occur.

Generally, it is noticeable that the more there are defaults on credits came from the lower the 'ProsperRating (Alpha)', but rating D is an exception as it tends to be the most frequent rating among defaulted credits.

Employment status 'Not employed' or 'Self-employed' for individuals, are more likely to have default on credit.

The long-term loans (60 months) have the greatest proportion of defaulted credits.

It is predictable that borrowers of high 'ProsperRating (Alpha)' will borrow more. In addition to, 'Defaulted' loans looks like to be larger than 'Completed' for high 'ProsperRating (Alpha)' borrowers. On the other hand, low 'ProsperRating (Alpha)' borrowers, we can safely say that, they borrow less and their defaulted credits, usually, as big as completed.

Also we can safely say that, 'Business' and 'Home Improvement' categories are the riskiest.

It can be witnessed that, higher 'StatedMonthlyIncome' came from individuals with high 'ProsperRating (Alpha)'.

'EmploymentStatus' with individuals ['Not employed', 'Self-employed', 'Retired'] are having lower 'ProsperRating (Alpha)'.
